What is making things much worse for Ukraine and easier for Russia is the widespread prevalence of apartment blocks that rely on communal central heating - where water is heated up elsewhere and then pumped into their radiators. Cities across the Soviet Union, including in Ukraine, were the focus of huge construction programmes launched in the 1950s to mass produce cheap housing. The landscapes of cities in the former USSR are dominated by ubiquitous nine-storey residential buildings made from pre-fabricated concrete panels, known as "panelki", or smaller five-storey blocks of flats known as "khrushchevki", after Soviet leader Nikita Khrushchev, who oversaw their construction in the 1950s and 1960s. The Ukrainian government is acutely aware of this vulnerability, and is planning to reduce it by making individual heating points mandatory at apartment blocks. However, undoing decades of Soviet urban planning will not be quick or easy.
